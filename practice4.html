Subscript and superscript tags are used in mathematical and scientific notation. Below are examples of their usage:

1. Mathematical Exponents

The square of x is written as: x2.

The cube of x is written as: x3.

2. Chemical Formulas

Water is represented as: H2O.

Carbon dioxide is represented as: CO2.

3. Series Representation

A series can be written as:

a1 + a2 + a3 + ... + an.

4. Units and Measurements

The formula for speed is: v = d/t, where:

  • d: distance (in meters, m)
  • t: time (in seconds, s)
  • v: velocity (in m/s)

5. Isotopes

An isotope of carbon is represented as: 14C.
